Hidden Cost

Brignull (2010) describes the pattern of a user going through multiple steps to checkout and after finally getting to the last step of the checkout process, discovering some unexpected charges have appeared, e.g. delivery charges, tax, etc. as ‘hidden costs’.

This occurs when the user goes through multiple steps in a checkout process and, when he gets to the end, he discovers new and unexpected costs were added to the total amount (fees, delivery costs, etc.).

Hidden costs have been around for as long as paid services, but they’re especially deceptive online. On the internet, many websites can keep these fees hidden until the last possible second and slap on an ambiguous label.

Fees aren’t always bad, but they become a bigger problem when they’re disclosed too late.
